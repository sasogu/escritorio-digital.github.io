# Digital Desktop – Widget Guide

Digital Desktop is a workboard with “windows” designed for the classroom. You can open the widgets you need, move them, resize them, and combine them to build your “work table” for an activity.

## How to use it

- **Open a widget**: from the bottom bar or from the **Widget Library**.
- **Close everything**: from the desktop context menu.
- **Profiles**: create several desktops and switch between them.
- **Backgrounds**: choose a preset background or upload your own.

## Widget catalog

### Organization and classroom

- **Task list**: task/reminder list. Useful for the day’s agenda, steps of a lab, quick rubric, or “session plan.”
- **Attendance**: quick attendance and class status record.
- **Group generator**: create groups randomly or balanced for cooperative work.
- **Calendar**: visual support for dates, deadlines, and planning.

### Time and rhythm

- **Timer**: countdown for stations, short exams, turn-based work, etc.
- **Stopwatch.
- **Metronome**: steady pulse. Useful for music, rhythm reading, class routines, or interval work.

### Math and science

- **Scientific calculator**: common calculations in science and math.
- **Unit converter for problems and labs.
- **Markdown + LaTeX.

### Whiteboard, writing, and resources

- **Notepad**: quick notes for instructions, ideas, examples, lists, etc.
- **Drawing pad.
- **Image carousel.
- **Web viewer**: open a web page inside the desktop to use an online resource without switching tabs.
- **PDF viewer**: open PDF files inside the desktop.
- **Live viewer**: designed to show web content or external resources comfortably while explaining.
- **HTML.

### Interaction, chance, and educational games

- **Dice**: quick rolls for randomness, gamification, or selection.
- **Random spinner**: pick turns, teams, questions, students, or challenges.
- **QR generator**: create QR codes to share links or resources with the class.
- **Traffic light**: visual signal for “silence / work / attention” or activity states.
- **Sound meter**: visual indicator of noise level for group self‑regulation.

### Logic games and class dynamics

- **Tic tac toe**: quick game for review, Q&A, motivation, or concept “duels.”
- **Memory**: matching pairs for vocabulary, concepts, images, and definitions.
- **Sliding puzzle**: puzzle for attention, logic, and transitions.

### Gesture-based work

- **Work gestures**: support tool for class routines based on signals/gestures.

## Classroom use ideas

- **Start of class**: Task list + Timer + Traffic Light.
- **Explanation**: Markdown + LaTeX + Drawing pad + Web viewer.
- **Cooperative work**: Group generator + Timer + Sound meter.
- **Gamification**: Random spinner + Dice + Scoreboard.
